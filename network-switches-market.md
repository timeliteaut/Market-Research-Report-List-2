<p><p>Network switches are networking devices that connect multiple devices, such as computers, printers, and servers, within a local area network (LAN). They operate at the data link layer of the OSI model and use MAC addresses to forward data to the appropriate destination device. Network switches provide increased network performance, security, and scalability compared to traditional hubs.</p><p>The increasing demand for high-speed internet connectivity, data center consolidation, and cloud computing services are driving the growth of the Network Switches Market. The market is also benefiting from the rise of Internet of Things (IoT) devices, which require reliable and secure network connections.</p><p>The Network Switches Market is expected to grow at a CAGR of 7.5% during the forecast period. <p><p>Network switches are categorized into three main types: Fixed Managed, Fixed Unmanaged, and Modular Switches. Fixed Managed switches offer advanced configuration and monitoring features, making them ideal for large networks. Fixed Unmanaged switches are plug-and-play devices that require no configuration, making them simple and cost-effective solutions for smaller networks. Modular switches offer flexibility and scalability by allowing users to customize their switch with interchangeable modules for different needs. Each type caters to specific networking requirements and budget considerations.</p></p>
